The primary objective of this project is to assess the Landslide Susceptibility levels of a section of the Lombardy region in Northern Italy. Landslide Susceptibility indicates the potential for landslide occurrences in a given area, represented by a qualitative range from "Low" to "Very High."

To develop this comprehensive measure, various foundational layers will be analyzed, including the Digital Terrain Model of the area, land use patterns, the presence of roads and rivers, among others. The ultimate goal is to identify areas most prone to landslides by evaluating the hazard levels associated with multiple territorial elements.

The presentation of the project is organized as follows:

  • Methodology: This section will detail the workflow and the various steps taken to achieve the final result.
  • Results: This section will present, discuss, and analyze the main findings.
  • WebGIS Integration: Finally, all the layers used and processed will be incorporated into a WebGIS for interactive map visualization.

THE AREA

The focus area includes the city of Como and the surrounding "convalle of Lake Como," extending from Brenno through Gradate to Nesso. This region experiences frequent landslides and flooding, warranting close monitoring by the Civil Protection. Notably, Blevio is highlighted as a high-risk area, particularly during heavy rainfall periods.
